What it does

One fader. Three databases. Infinite taste.

StreamFader pulls live data from three sources — Rotten Tomatoes, Metacritic, and IMDB — and puts you in control of how they're weighted. The crossfader sits at the top of the interface. Drag it. Everything below reranks in real time.

All the way left, you're watching what critics agree on. All the way right, you're watching what audiences love. Somewhere in the middle is usually the truth.

  • Critic score: Rotten Tomatoes + Metacritic averaged into one number
  • Audience score: IMDB rating scaled to 100
  • Real-time blend driven by your fader position
  • Genre filter pills: Drama, Comedy, Thriller, Horror, Sci-Fi, and more
  • Card grid with posters, scores, and streaming metadata
  • 6-hour cache so the data stays fresh without hammering the APIs

Why it exists

Critics and audiences don't always agree. That's useful information.

A film with 90% on RT and 6.2 on IMDB is telling you something. So is one with 45% from critics and an 8.1 from audiences. Conventional recommendation engines hide that tension. StreamFader puts it in your hands.

If you're in the mood to be challenged, pull left. If you want to watch something you'll actually enjoy, pull right. The scoring gap between left and right is the signal worth paying attention to.

How to use it

Open, slide, watch.

Open StreamFader in your browser. The crossfader defaults to a slight audience lean — a reasonable starting point. Browse the card grid, filter by genre, or adjust the fader until the list feels right.

No account required. No preferences to configure. StreamFader is free and works on any device with a browser.
